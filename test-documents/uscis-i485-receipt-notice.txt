IMPORTANT — EMPLOYMENT AUTHORIZATION:
If you filed Form I-765 (Employment Authorization Document) concurrently
with your I-485, you may be eligible to work while your I-485 is pending.
You will receive a separate notice regarding your I-765.

If your I-765 is approved, you will receive an Employment Authorization
Document (EAD card) by mail. Do NOT begin working until you have received
and activated your EAD card.

TRAVEL WARNING — READ CAREFULLY

If you travel outside the United States while your I-485 is pending, your
application may be considered ABANDONED and denied, UNLESS you obtain
Advance Parole (Form I-131) before departing. If you have already filed
Form I-131 concurrently, do not travel until it is approved and you have
received your travel document.

MAINTAINING YOUR ADDRESS ON FILE

You are required by law to notify USCIS within 10 days if you change your
address. Failure to do so may result in missed notices and denial of your
application. Update your address at: uscis.gov/addresschange or by filing
Form AR-11 (Alien's Change of Address Card).

AVOID IMMIGRATION SCAMS

Only attorneys licensed to practice law or accredited representatives
recognized by the Board of Immigration Appeals (BIA) may provide immigration
legal advice. Notarios, immigration consultants, and document preparers
who are NOT attorneys CANNOT give you legal advice. Beware of anyone who
guarantees a specific result or charges excessive fees.
